# InControl Lesson Generator — How to Use
### For Nick (Content Lead)

---

## What this tool does

You fill in a brief about the lesson you want to make. The AI generates all 8 parts of the lesson for you. You read through each part, make any edits, add the video, approve everything, and publish. Done.

The lesson then shows up in the app that learners use.

## Step 1 — Fill out the brief

The brief is the form that tells the AI what kind of lesson to make. The better your brief, the better the lesson.

**Go to the Brief tab.** Fill in these fields:

| Field | What to put |
|---|---|
| **Lesson title** | The name of the lesson. Short and clear. e.g. "What Is a Resistance Band?" |
| **Skill area** | The general topic. e.g. "Resistance Training" |
| **Domain** | Pick from the dropdown. Usually matches the skill area. |
| **Month** | When in the program this lesson appears. Pick Intro, M1, M2, M3, or X. |
| **Learning objective** | One sentence: what will the learner be able to DO after this lesson. e.g. "Learner can name two reasons resistance training helps their body." |
| **Real-world anchor** | A real-life moment this lesson connects to. e.g. "Getting up from a chair" or "Carrying groceries." |
| **Learner long-term goal** | The learner's personal goal. e.g. "Feel stronger and keep up with friends." |
| **Daily routine anchors** | 2–3 things the learner already does every day. Type each one and press Enter. e.g. "Making breakfast" / "Brushing teeth" / "Getting dressed." |
| **Special notes** | Anything the AI should know. e.g. "Avoid mentioning weight or calories. Keep all language positive." |

When you're done, click **Generate Lesson with Claude →**

---

## Step 2 — Wait for the lesson to generate

The AI usually takes 10–20 seconds. You'll see a loading indicator.

When it's done, 8 step cards appear — one for each part of the lesson.

---

## Step 3 — Review each step card

Each card shows the AI's draft for that part of the lesson. Go through them one at a time.

**Click on a card to expand it** and read the content.

**What to check:**
- Does the language sound like InControl? Warm, simple, encouraging?
- Is the reading level simple enough? Short sentences. Plain words.
- Are the choices (for the practice steps) all real options — no trick answers?
- Does the commitment in Step 7 use one of the daily routines you listed in the brief?
- Is the celebration praise specific to THIS lesson, not just "Great job!"?

**If something needs to change:**

*Option A — Edit it yourself:* Click into the text and type your changes directly.

*Option B — Ask the AI to redo it:* Click **Edit & Regenerate** on that card. A box pops up. Type what needs to be different (e.g. "Make the question simpler" or "Change the choices — they're too similar to each other"). Click Regenerate. The AI rewrites just that card.

---

## Step 4 — Add the video to Step 3 (Teach)

Step 3 (the Teach card) includes a **Video Production Plan** — a script and description for whoever is filming the video.

Once the video is filmed:

**To add a YouTube link:**
1. Paste the YouTube URL into the box that says "Paste YouTube URL..."
2. Click **Save link**
3. The video will embed right in the card

**To upload a video file:**
1. Click **Upload video file**
2. Select your mp4 or mov file
3. It uploads automatically and shows in the card

If the video isn't ready yet, leave it blank and come back later. You can always add or swap the video after publishing.

---

## Step 5 — Approve each card

When you're happy with a card, click **Approve ✓** at the bottom of it.

The card turns green and collapses.

Do this for all 8 cards.

You can unapprove and re-edit any card at any time before publishing.

---

## Step 6 — Publish

When all 8 cards are approved, the **Publish lesson** button at the bottom turns active.

Click it. The lesson saves to the database and becomes available in the learner app.

---

## Step 7 — Check the Library

Click the **Library** tab to see all lessons.

Your new lesson will appear at the top of the list with status "Published."

Click any lesson to open it and read through it again.

## Tips for writing a good brief

**The learning objective is the most important field.**  
Write it as a behavior, not a topic. Not "learn about hydration" — instead: "Learner can name one time of day to drink a glass of water and say why."

**Daily routine anchors make or break Step 7.**  
The commitment step only works if the anchor is something real the learner already does every day. If you put vague routines, the commitment will be vague. Be specific: "Making breakfast" not "morning routine."

**Use Special Notes for anything sensitive.**  
If there are words or topics to avoid (weight, calories, "diet"), put them here. If there's a particular tone or voice you want, describe it here.

**One concept per lesson.**  
Don't try to cover three things in one lesson. If you have three concepts, make three lessons. The AI will try to follow your objective, so a focused objective gives you a focused lesson.

## What to do if something goes wrong

**The lesson didn't generate / shows an error:**  
Try clicking Regenerate All. If it keeps failing, let Shannon know.

**A step card looks wrong or off-brand:**  
Edit it yourself or use Edit & Regenerate with specific feedback about what needs to change.

**You published a lesson but need to change something:**  
Let Shannon know — changes to published lessons need to go through the tool and re-publish.
