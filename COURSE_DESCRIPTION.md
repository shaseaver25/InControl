# InControl — Course Description

**Version:** Beta 1–3 (current)  
**Population:** Teens and adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD), autism spectrum disorder (ASD), and mixed-needs profiles  
**Delivery platform:** Trainerize (workout delivery) + InControl lesson app (education and coaching)  
**Format:** Home-based, coach-supported, self-paced  

---

## What InControl teaches

InControl is a structured fitness and health behavior curriculum that builds independent exercise habits in people with IDD and ASD. The curriculum combines explicit skill instruction, adaptive movement, and self-determination practices to move learners from supported participation toward autonomous health behavior.

The course is not a workout program. It is a behavior-change and skill-building program delivered *through* a workout program. The goal is not a specific fitness outcome — it is that learners independently choose, initiate, and sustain healthy behaviors because those behaviors connect to their own goals.

## Nine domains

### 1. App Navigation & Onboarding (11 concepts)
Opening Trainerize, finding workouts, logging reps, messaging the coach, completing tasks, earning bonus points. Goal: **independently use the app.**

### 2. Movement Foundations & Safety (8 concepts)
The "First Five" pre-workout routine (gather materials, clear space, check safety, estimate time, body-part vocabulary), counting reps, understanding sets, progressive overload, safe stretching rules. Foundational to all other domains. Cross-cutting throughout the curriculum.

### 3. Body Awareness & Self-Modification (9 concepts)
Listening to body signals, judging difficulty (too hard / too easy / just right), using the 3-tier intensity system (level 1/2/3), choosing resistance bands and household weights, identifying where a movement is felt, matching exercise to target muscle, using modifications. Goal: **self-select the right intensity.**

### 4. Resistance Training (38 concepts + skills)
What resistance training is and why it matters, resistance band mechanics (tension, color coding, combining bands), bodyweight training, household-item weights (backpacks, bottles, cans, laundry), core training (belly/butt/back). Specific exercises: Squat, Row, Chest Press, Shoulder Press, Lateral Raise, Bicep Curl, Calf Raise, Pallof Press, Push-Up, Plank, Wall Sit, Glute Bridge, Lunge, Chair Dip, Backpack Squat, Water Bottle Press, Detergent Curl, Laundry Deadlift, Hip Hinge, Good Morning, Russian Twist, and others. Goal: **build and complete a resistance workout.**

### 5. Flexibility & Mobility (34 concepts + skills)
Difference between static and dynamic stretching, warm-up vs. cooldown purpose and structure, mobility drills, yoga-adjacent movements. Specific exercises: Forward Fold, Quad Stretch, Hip Flexor Stretch, Child's Pose, Cat-Cow, Standing Hip Circle, Ankle Circle, Wrist Circle, Neck Roll, Shoulder Roll, T-Spine Rotation, Lateral Lunge, Pigeon Pose (modified), Figure-4 stretch, and others. Goal: **build a warm-up and cooldown routine.**

### 6. Cardiovascular & Endurance (11 concepts)
What cardiovascular exercise is, why it matters, interval training basics, how to find and maintain a target effort level, specific cardio movements (Marching in Place, Step Touch, High Knees, Jumping Jacks, modified versions). Goal: **build cardiovascular endurance.**

### 7. Coordination & Balance (8 concepts)
Balance fundamentals, unilateral stance, hop patterns, throw-and-catch patterns. Goal: **improve coordination and balance.**

### 8. Real-World Transfer (4 concepts)
Applying fitness skills to daily living activities — carrying groceries, getting up from the floor, walking longer distances, navigating stairs. Goal: **apply fitness to independent daily living.**

### 9. Self-Regulation & Wellbeing (7 concepts)
Energy check-ins, distinguishing rest from push days, the 3×/week exercise cap and "talk to someone" escalation, post-workout reflection, peer and partner support. Goal: **sustain a consistent exercise habit.**

## Eight learning goals

These are the right-side anchors in the learning graph — the measurable outcomes the curriculum is designed to produce:

1. Independently use the Trainerize app
2. Self-select the right intensity for any exercise
3. Build and complete a resistance workout
4. Build a warm-up and cooldown routine
5. Build cardiovascular endurance
6. Improve coordination and balance
7. Apply fitness skills to independent daily living
8. Sustain a consistent exercise habit

---

## Adaptive design

**75 of 133 concepts are marked modifiable** (`modifiable = Y`) — meaning each has at least one easier and one harder variant. Modifications cover: seated vs. standing, supported vs. unsupported, reduced range of motion, household substitutes for equipment, and cognitive load reductions.

The variation model (documented in `InControl Learning Graph — Playbook.md`) keeps modifications in a separate variant layer so the core concept graph stays stable. A learner's capability profile, mastery state, and daily energy level determine which variant is delivered — not a different graph.

**Contraindications** are hard gates. An exercise flagged as contraindicated for a given capability profile is never shown, regardless of other factors.

---

## What the curriculum is not

- It is not a clinical intervention or a medical program.
- It is not a diet or nutrition program (nutrition content may be added in a future phase).
- It is not a one-size program — every concept has modification paths and the sequencing adapts to what each learner already knows.
- It is not designed to be completed in a fixed number of weeks — the pacing is learner-driven within the month structure.
